How I Validate

A practical framework for testing ideas with rigor — without overcomplicating the process. Tailored to your traffic, tooling, and timeline.

1) Diagnose the opportunity

Start with what’s actually happening: analytics signals, funnel drop-off, UX friction, and customer feedback. We align on the problem before jumping to solutions.

2) Write a testable hypothesis

Turn ideas into clear hypotheses tied to behavior: what will change, for whom, and why. Define success metrics and guardrails upfront.

3) Validate + decide

Run an A/B test when volume supports it. If it doesn’t, use a staged rollout or pre/post validation with clear decision criteria — so we still learn and move forward confidently.

How I work with design + creative

I partner closely with design teams to validate concepts before they’re permanently shipped — testing variations, measuring impact across the journey, and identifying what to scale vs. iterate.

Test Concept: Countdown + urgency messaging at a high-intent moment.

Scenario: Teams needed a clear way to communicate a time-sensitive promotion without overwhelming the page or hurting downstream conversion.

My Role: Designed the test plan, aligned on success metrics and guardrails, partnered with design and development on the UI pattern, and monitored performance daily to inform rollout decisions.

Contextual Discovery Framework Lift in cross-sell engagement • Guardrails held

Test Concept: Cross-sell placement within product discovery.

Scenario: Users browsing category and product pathways often didn’t discover relevant add-ons until late in the journey, missing earlier opportunities to engage.

Hypothesis: If we introduce a contextual cross-sell card where intent is actively forming, we can increase cross-sell engagement without disrupting the primary browse goal.

My Role: Mapped the placement to the funnel stage, defined eligibility logic, aligned tracking requirements, and validated impact beyond clicks by monitoring progression and conversion guardrails.

Wayfinding & Navigation Framework Increased engagement • Improved progression

Test Concept: Navigation and wayfinding improvement at key decision points.

Scenario: Users weren’t finding high-value pages quickly, creating drop-off, repeated backtracking, and missed opportunities to self-serve into the next best step.

Hypothesis: If we add a clearer navigation element with more purposeful labels, more users will move into key pages and show stronger downstream progression.

My Role: Defined the behavioral goals, established success metrics across both engagement and downstream movement, and ensured the results were interpreted correctly as a net-new navigational aid rather than inflated “increase” framing.

Outcome: Increased engagement with the new navigation element and improved movement into key content areas.
