How I Validate

A practical approach I use to shape, prioritize, and validate experiments so decisions are tied to meaningful outcomes.

1. Diagnose the opportunity

Start with what is actually happening. Look at analytics, funnel behavior, UX friction, and customer signals so the team is solving the right problem.

2. Write a testable hypothesis

Turn an idea into a clear hypothesis tied to behavior. Define what changes, who it is for, and what success looks like before execution begins.

3. Validate and decide

Use the right validation method for the traffic, risk, and timeline. That may be an A B test, a staged rollout, or a more directional learning plan with defined guardrails.

How I work with design and creative

I work closely with design teams to validate ideas before they become permanent. That means testing variations, measuring downstream impact, and knowing what should scale versus what should keep evolving.

Behavioral Urgency Framework Double digit lift in CTR and a scalable pattern

Test Concept: Countdown messaging placed at a high intent moment.

Scenario: The team needed a simple way to communicate a limited time promotion without cluttering the page or hurting downstream conversion.

Hypothesis: If we place a clear countdown near the primary CTA and tighten the urgency copy, engagement will increase while downstream performance remains stable or improves.

My Role: I built the test plan, aligned success metrics and guardrails, partnered with design and development on the pattern, and monitored performance daily to support rollout decisions.

Outcome: The test drove a meaningful lift in engagement and became a reusable pattern for major promotional moments.

Contextual Discovery Framework Lift in cross sell engagement while guardrails held

Test Concept: Contextual product recommendation within phone discovery.

Scenario: Users browsing phones were not always seeing relevant companion products early enough in the journey, which limited discovery and cross sell engagement.

Hypothesis: If we surface a more relevant companion product in the discovery flow based on account and ecosystem context, engagement will improve without disrupting the primary browse goal.

My Role: I mapped the placement to the funnel stage, defined the eligibility logic, aligned tracking needs, and evaluated performance beyond clicks by checking progression and conversion guardrails.

Outcome: The pattern increased cross sell engagement with no meaningful negative impact to the primary conversion goal.

Wayfinding and Navigation Framework Higher engagement and stronger progression

Test Concept: Navigation and wayfinding improvements at key decision points.

Scenario: Users were not finding high value pages quickly, which created drop off, backtracking, and missed opportunities to move into the next best step.

Hypothesis: If we introduce a clearer navigation element with more purposeful labels, more users will reach key pages and show stronger downstream progression.

My Role: I defined the behavioral goals, set the success metrics across engagement and downstream movement, and made sure the results were interpreted as a net new navigational aid rather than inflated performance language.

Outcome: The new pattern increased engagement with the navigation element and improved movement into key content areas.

Decision Clarity Framework Improved progression and less hesitation

Test Concept: Offer and plan messaging at the moment of decision.

Scenario: Users hesitated when comparing options because the value proposition and recommended path were not immediately clear.

Hypothesis: If we simplify the message and make the most relevant path easier to understand, hesitation will drop and progression to the next step will improve.

My Role: I shaped the hypothesis, aligned stakeholders around success criteria, translated the business question into measurable behavior, and helped define the pattern that made the recommended choice clearer.

Outcome: The test improved engagement with the offer and increased progression into the next stage of the funnel.
