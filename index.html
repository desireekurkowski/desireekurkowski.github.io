How I Validate

A practical framework for testing ideas with rigor — without overcomplicating the process. Tailored to your traffic, tooling, and timeline.

1) Diagnose the opportunity

Start with what’s actually happening: analytics signals, funnel drop-off, UX friction, and customer feedback. We align on the problem before jumping to solutions.

2) Write a testable hypothesis

Turn ideas into clear hypotheses tied to behavior: what will change, for whom, and why. Define success metrics and guardrails upfront.

3) Validate + decide

Run an A/B test when volume supports it. If it doesn’t, use a staged rollout or pre/post validation with clear decision criteria — so we still learn and move forward confidently.

How I work with design + creative

I partner closely with design teams to validate concepts before they’re permanently shipped — testing variations, measuring impact across the journey, and identifying what to scale vs. iterate.

▾ Behavioral Urgency Framework
Double-digit lift in CTR • Scaled pattern

Test / Concept: Countdown + urgency messaging at a high-intent moment

Scenario: Teams needed a clear way to communicate a time-sensitive promotion without overwhelming the page or hurting conversion.

Hypothesis: If we add a simple countdown and tighten urgency copy near the primary CTA, engagement will increase (CTR) while downstream conversion holds steady or improves.

What I did: Designed the test plan, aligned on success metrics + guardrails, partnered with design/dev on the UI, and monitored performance daily to inform rollout decisions.

Outcome: Double-digit lift in engagement (CTR). Scaled as a reusable pattern for major site moments.

▾ Contextual Discovery Framework
Lift in cross-sell engagement • Guardrails held

Test / Concept: Cross-sell placement in product discovery

Scenario: Users browsing a category page often didn’t discover relevant add-ons until late in the journey.

Hypothesis: If we introduce a contextual cross-sell card in the discovery flow (where intent is forming), we can increase cross-sell engagement without disrupting the primary browse goal.

What I did: Mapped placement to funnel stage, defined eligibility rules, aligned tracking, and validated impact beyond clicks (progression + conversion guardrails).

Outcome: Lift in cross-sell engagement with no meaningful negative impact to the primary conversion goal.

▾ Wayfinding & Navigation Framework
Higher engagement • Improved progression

Test / Concept: Navigation + wayfinding improvement

Scenario: Users weren’t finding high-value pages quickly, creating drop-off and repeated backtracking.

Hypothesis: If we add a clearer navigation element with purposeful labels, more users will self-serve and move into key pages (higher CTR + stronger downstream progression).

What I did: Defined behavioral goals, set success metrics (CTR + downstream), and ensured results were interpreted correctly (net-new element vs “increase” framing).

Outcome: Increased engagement with the new navigation element and improved movement into key content.

▾ Decision Clarity Framework
Reduced hesitation • Stronger funnel progression

Test / Concept: Offer messaging at the decision point

Scenario: Users hesitated when comparing options because the value proposition wasn’t immediately clear.

Hypothesis: If we simplify the message and surface the “why it matters” at the decision point, we can reduce hesitation and improve progression.

What I did: Crafted the hypothesis, aligned stakeholders on what success means, and translated the business question into measurable user behavior.
